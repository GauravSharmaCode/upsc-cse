# 2026-03-28 — The Hindu — Governor's Power to Withhold Assent

## **Summary**

The Supreme Court, in a recent judgment, has further clarified the limits of the Governor's power under **Article 200** of the Constitution. The Court emphasized that a Governor cannot keep a bill pending indefinitely and must exercise their options "as soon as possible" if they choose to withhold assent.

## **Why it matters**

- **Federalism**: Frequent friction between State Governments and Governors over legislative delays affects the democratic process.
- **Constitutional Clarity**: Clarifies the phrase "as soon as possible" in the first proviso of Article 200.
- **Prelims Focus**: Article 200 (options for Governor), Article 201 (Presidential assent), and the discretionary powers of the Governor.

## **Key Facts for Prelims**

- **Article 200**: Options for a Governor when a bill is presented:
    1. Give **Assent**.
    2. **Withhold** Assent.
    3. **Return** the bill (if not a Money Bill) for reconsideration.
    4. **Reserve** the bill for the consideration of the President.
- If the bill is passed again by the State Legislature (with or without amendments), the Governor **must** give assent.
- **Sarkaria Commission** & **Punchhi Commission** both recommended time-bound disposal of bills by the Governor.
