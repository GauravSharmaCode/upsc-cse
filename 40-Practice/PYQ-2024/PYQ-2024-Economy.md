## Q33. CBDC — Central Bank Digital Currency (E/FCA)

- (b) 2 only

## Q82. Government Securities — G-Sec (E/F)

Consider the following about Government Securities (G-Secs):
- 1. They are tradeable instruments issued by the Central Government
- 2. They carry sovereign (zero default) risk
- 3. They are used by RBI for Open Market Operations

- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- **(d) All three** ✅

**VisionIAS Explanation:** G-Secs are tradeable debt instruments issued by Central/State Governments to meet fiscal deficits. They carry sovereign guarantee (zero default risk). RBI uses them in OMOs to control liquidity. T-bills are short-term G-Secs (up to 364 days); Dated Securities are long-term.
