## Q3. Post-Independence — Political Parties (D/F)

Consider the following pairs:
**Party : Leader**
1. Bhartiya Jana Sangh : Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherjee
2. Socialist Party : C. Rajagopalachari
3. Congress for Democracy : Jagjivan Ram
4. Swatantra Party : Acharya Narendra Dev

How many of the above are correctly matched?

- (a) Only one
- **(b) Only two** ✅
- (c) Only three
- (d) All four

**Why correct:**
> - **Pair 1 is correct:** Bhartiya Jana Sangh was established in 1951 by Dr. Shyama Prasad Mukherjee.
> - **Pair 2 is incorrect:** Socialist Party was associated with leaders like Ram Manohar Lohia and Jayaprakash Narayan. C. Rajagopalachari founded the Swatantra Party.
> - **Pair 3 is correct:** Congress for Democracy was formed in 1977 by Jagjivan Ram.
> - **Pair 4 is incorrect:** Swatantra Party was founded by C. Rajagopalachari. Acharya Narendra Dev was a key leader of the Congress Socialist Party (CSP).

## Q12. GOI Act 1935 — Provisions (E/F)

With reference to the Government of India Act, 1935, consider the following statements:
1. It provided for the establishment of an All India Federation based on the union of British Indian Provinces and Princely States.
2. Defence and Foreign Affairs were kept under the control of the federal legislature.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- **(a) 1 only** ✅
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> Statement 1 is correct as the Act proposed an All India Federation. Statement 2 is incorrect because Defence and External Affairs were 'Reserved' subjects under the control of the Governor-General, not the federal legislature.

## Q13. Bhasa — Sanskrit Dramatist (D/F)

Which one of the following is a work attributed to playwright Bhasa?
- (a) Kavyalankara
- (b) Natyashastra
- **(c) Madhyama-vyayoga** ✅
- (d) Mahabhashya

**Why correct:**
> Bhasa was one of the earliest Sanskrit dramatists. His works include Madhyama-vyayoga, Swapnavasavadatta, and Charudatta. Kavyalankara is by Bhamaha, Natyashastra by Bharata Muni, and Mahabhashya by Patanjali.

## Q14. Sanghabhuti — Buddhist Monk (D/F)

Sanghabhuti, an Indian Buddhist monk, travelled to China at the end of the fourth century AD, was the author of a commentary on:
- (a) Prajnaparamita Sutra
- (b) Visuddhimagga
- **(c) Sarvastivada Vinaya** ✅
- (d) Lalitavistara

**Why correct:**
> Sanghabhuti was a Kashmiri monk who went to China and translated Buddhist texts. He is noted for his commentary on the Sarvastivada Vinaya.

## Q15. UNESCO Recognitions 2023 (E/FCA)

Consider the following properties included in the World Heritage List released by UNESCO:
1. Santiniketan
2. Rani-ki-Vav
3. Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas
4. Mahabodhi Temple Complex at Bodhgaya

How many of the above properties were included in 2023?

- (a) Only one
- **(b) Only two** ✅
- (c) Only three
- (d) All four

**Why correct:**
> Santiniketan (West Bengal) and the Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas (Karnataka) were added to the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2023. Rani-ki-Vav was added in 2014 and Mahabodhi Temple in 2002.

## Q34. Ancient India — Buddha Epithets (M/F)

With reference to ancient India, Gautama Buddha was generally known by which of the following epithets?
1. Nayaputta
2. Shakyamuni
3. Tathagata

Select the correct answer using the code given below:
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 and 3 only** ✅
- (c) 1, 2 and 3
- (d) None of the above

**Why correct:**
> Gautama Buddha is known as **Shakyamuni** (Sage of the Shakyas) and **Tathagata** (One who has thus gone/come). **Nayaputta** (or Nataputta) refers to Vardhamana Mahavira.

## Q35. Ancient Sites — Matching (D/F)

Consider the following information:
**Archaeological Site : State Description**
1. Chandraketugarh : Odisha Trading Port town
2. Inamgaon : Maharashtra Chalcolithic site
3. Mangadu : Kerala Megalithic site
4. Salihundam : Andhra Pradesh Rock-cut cave shrines

In which of the above rows is given information correctly matched?
- (a) 1 and 2
- **(b) 2 and 3** ✅
- (c) 3 and 4
- (d) 1 and 4

**Why correct:**
> - **Row 1 is incorrect:** Chandraketugarh is in West Bengal (Ganges Delta).

## Q37. Ryotwari Settlement — Features (E/F)

With reference to revenue collection by Cornwallis, consider the following statements:
1. Under the Ryotwari Settlement, peasants were exempted from revenue payment in case of bad harvests or natural calamities.
2. Under the Permanent Settlement in Bengal, if a Zamindar failed to pay his revenues to the state on or before the fixed date, their land would auctioned off.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✅
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> - Statement 1 is incorrect: Under Ryotwari, there was no general provision for exemption due to bad harvests; it was quite rigid. 
> - Statement 2 is correct: The 'Sunset Law' of the Permanent Settlement mandated that if the revenue was not paid by sunset of the specified date, the zamindari would be auctioned.

## Q38. Upanishads vs Puranas (M/FA)

Consider the following statements:
1. There are no parables in Upanishads.
2. Upanishads were composed earlier than the Puranas.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✅
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> - Statement 1 is incorrect: Upanishads contain many parables (e.g., Satyakama Jabala).
> - Statement 2 is correct: Upanishads (Vedic period) were composed much earlier than the Puranas (Post-Mauryan/Gupta period).
