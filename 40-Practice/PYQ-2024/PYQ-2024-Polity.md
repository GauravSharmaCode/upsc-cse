## Q23. Writ of Prohibition (E/F)

A Writ of Prohibition is issued by a higher court to a lower court:

- (a) To quash an order already passed
- (b) To produce a detained person
- **(c) To stop proceedings when acting beyond jurisdiction** ✅
- (d) To direct a public authority to perform a duty

**VisionIAS Explanation:** Prohibition is issued to prevent a lower court/tribunal from exceeding its jurisdiction or acting contrary to natural justice. Unlike Certiorari (which quashes a decision already made), Prohibition stops proceedings before they are completed.

**Why correct:**
> A Writ of Prohibition is issued to prevent a lower court or tribunal from exceeding its jurisdiction. Unlike Certiorari, which quashes an order already passed, Prohibition stops proceedings before they are completed. It is available only against judicial and quasi-judicial authorities.
