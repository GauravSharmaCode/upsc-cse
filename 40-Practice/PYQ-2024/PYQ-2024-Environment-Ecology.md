## Q68. Fig Trees — Symbiotic Relationship (E/F)

Which one of the following best describes the relationship between fig trees and fig wasps?

- **(a) Mutualism** ✅
- (b) Commensalism
- (c) Parasitism
- (d) Predation

**VisionIAS Explanation:** In many species of fig trees, there is a tight one-to-one relationship with specific fig wasp species — each depends on the other for reproduction. The wasp pollinates the fig while laying eggs inside it. This is a classic example of mutualism (obligate symbiosis).

- 3. It produces zero carbon emissions compared to conventional jet fuel

- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- **(c) 1 and 2 only** ✅

**VisionIAS Explanation:** SAF IS used in commercial aviation and CAN be produced from waste biomass, agricultural residues, etc. However, it does NOT produce zero emissions — it reduces lifecycle carbon emissions by up to 80% compared to conventional jet fuel, but is not zero-carbon.
