## Q9. Longest International Border (E/F)

## Q17. Declining Population Countries (D/CA)

Which of the following countries are frequently mentioned in the media for their low birth rates, or ageing population of declining population?
1. Italy
2. Japan
3. Nigeria
4. South Korea
5. South Africa

Select the correct answer:
- **(a) 1, 2 and 4** ✅
- (b) 1, 3 and 5
- (c) 2 and 4 only
- (d) 3 and 5 only

**Why correct:**
> Japan, Italy, and South Korea are well-known for their demographic crises, extremely low fertility rates (South Korea at ~0.7), and ageing populations. Nigeria and South Africa have relatively younger populations and higher birth rates.

## Q29. Red Sea Region — Features (D/FA)

Consider the following statements:
1. The Red Sea region receives very little precipitation in any form.
2. No water enters the Red Sea from rivers.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- **(a) 1 only** ✅
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> - Statement 1 is correct: The Red Sea region is extremely arid with very little rainfall.
> - Statement 2 is incorrect: While there are no *major* perennial rivers, small rivulets/wadis do drain into it during rare rain events. (VisionIAS notes this distinction).

## Q51. Atmospheric Heating (E/F)

Consider the following statements:
Statement-I: The atmosphere is heated more by incoming solar radiation than by terrestrial radiation.
Statement-II: Carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases in the atmosphere are good absorbers of long wave radiation.

Which one of the following is correct?
- (a) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I
- (b) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct but Statement-II does not explain Statement-I
- (c) Statement-I is correct but Statement-II is incorrect
- **(d) Statement-I is incorrect, but Statement-II is correct** ✅

**Why correct:**
> Statement-I is incorrect because the atmosphere is largely transparent to short-wave solar radiation and is primarily heated from below by long-wave terrestrial radiation. Statement-II is correct as GHGs are indeed good absorbers of long-wave radiation.

## Q54. Isotherms Deviations (E/F)

Which of the following is/are correct inference/inferences from isothermal maps in the month of January?
1. The isotherms deviate to the north over the ocean and to the south over the continent in the northern hemisphere.
2. The presence of warm ocean currents, Gulf Stream and North Atlantic Drift make the North Atlantic Ocean warmer and the isotherms bend towards the north.

Select the answer:
- **(a) 1 only** ✅
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> In January (winter in Northern Hemisphere), land is colder than the ocean. Isotherms bend poleward (North) over oceans and equatorward (South) over land. Statement 2 is also correct in principle but check official key vs VisionIAS for specific January trends. (VisionIAS marks A).

## Q56. Himalayan Rivers Sequence (M/F)

With reference to the Himalayan rivers joining the Ganga downstream of Prayagraj from West to East, which one of the following sequences is correct?
- (a) Ghaghara - Gomati - Gandak - Kosi
- **(b) Gomati - Ghaghara - Gandak - Kosi** ✅
- (c) Ghaghara - Gomati - Kosi - Gandak

Consider the following information:
**Waterfall : Region : River**
1. Dhuandhar : Malwa : Narmada
2. Hundru : Chota Nagpur : Subarnarekha
3. Gersoppa : Western Ghats : Netravati

In how many of the above rows is the given information correctly matched?
- **(a) Only one pair** ✅
- (b) Only two
- (c) All three
- (d) None

**Why correct:**
> - **Row 1 is incorrect:** Dhuandhar is on Narmada, but in the Mahakoshal region (Jabalpur), not Malwa.
> - **Row 2 is correct:** Hundru falls are on the Subarnarekha river in the Chota Nagpur plateau.
> - **Row 3 is incorrect:** Gersoppa (Jog) falls are on the Sharavathi river, not Netravati.

## Q72. Water Vapour (E/F)

With reference to "water vapour", which of the following statements is/are correct?

- **(a) 1 only** ✅
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2

> Water vapour decreases with altitude and also decreases from the equator towards the poles. It is maximum in the humid tropics.

## Q74. Coriolis Force (E/F)

With reference to "Coriolis force", which of the following statements is/are correct?
1. It increases with increase in wind velocity.
2. It is maximum at the poles and is absent at the equator.

Select the correct answer:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- **(c) Both 1 and 2** ✅
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

**Why correct:**
> Coriolis force is directly proportional to the angle of latitude (sin theta) and wind velocity. It is zero at the equator and maximum at the poles.

## Q75. Sunlight on June 21 (E/F)

On June 21 every year, which of the following latitude(s) experience(s) a sunlight of more than 12 hours?
1. Equator
2. Tropic of Cancer
3. Tropic of Capricorn
4. Arctic Circle

Select the answer:
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- (c) 3 and 4
- **(d) 1, 2 and 4** ✅

**Why correct:**
> On Summer Solstice, all places in the Northern Hemisphere (including Equator which has 12h always, but slightly more due to refraction) have days longer than 12 hours. Arctic Circle has 24 hours.

## Q81. Total Fertility Rate (E/F)

The total fertility rate in a economy is defined as:
- (a) the number of children born per 1000 people in the population in a year.
- (b) the number of children born to couple in their lifetime in a given population.
- (c) the birth rate minus death rate.
- **(d) the average number of live births a woman would have by the end of her child-bearing age.** ✅

**Why correct:**
> TFR is the average number of children that would be born to a woman over her lifetime if she were to experience the current age-specific fertility rates.
