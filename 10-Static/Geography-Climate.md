# Geography - Climate

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
Climate is the long-term average of weather conditions in a given area, typically over a period of 30 years or more. While weather describes short-term atmospheric changes, climate defines the overall character of a region's environment.

## Key Facts

- **Continentality**: Areas far from the sea (interiors) experience extreme temperature ranges (hot summers, cold winters).
- **Altitude**: Temperature decreases with height at a rate of **6.5°C per 1,000m** (Normal Lapse Rate).
- **Latitude**: The amount of solar radiation (insolation) decreases from the equator toward the poles.
- **Ocean Currents**: Warm currents (e.g., Gulf Stream) raise coastal temperatures; cold currents (e.g., Benguela) lower them and often create deserts.
- **Relief**: Mountains act as barriers; windward sides receive heavy rain (orographic), while leeward sides remain dry (rain-shadow).

## Details / Notes

### Weather vs. Climate
- **Weather**: Short-term state of the atmosphere (e.g., sunny today, rainy tomorrow).
- **Climate**: Long-term pattern and average of weather (e.g., Tropical, Arid, Tundra).

### Factors Influencing Climate (LAPTOP)
1. **L - Latitude**: Primary determinant of temperature. Equatorial regions are hot; Polar regions are cold.
2. **A - Altitude**: Higher altitudes are cooler. Shimla is cooler than Ludhiana despite being at similar latitudes.
3. **P - Pressure and Winds**: High pressure to low pressure movement. Monsoon winds in India are a classic example.
4. **T - Distance from Sea (Temperature Range)**: Maritime climate (moderate) vs. Continental climate (extreme).
5. **O - Ocean Currents**: Warm and cold currents modify coastal climates.
6. **P - Precipitation and Relief**: High mountains force air to rise, cool, and condense (Orographic Rainfall).

### Elements of Climate
- **Temperature**: Measured by thermometer.
- **Atmospheric Pressure**: Measured by barometer.
- **Wind**: Speed (anemometer) and direction (wind vane).
- **Moisture/Humidity**: Amount of water vapor in the air.
- **Precipitation**: Rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
