# Polity - Making of the Constitution

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
The Indian Constitution was drafted by the Constituent Assembly between 1946 and 1949. It was established based on the recommendations of the **Cabinet Mission Plan (1946)** and remains one of the longest written constitutions in the world.

## Key points

- **Cabinet Mission (1946)**: Provided the scheme for the Constituent Assembly.
- **Dr. Rajendra Prasad**: Permanent President of the Assembly (Sachchidananda Sinha was temporary).
- **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar**: Chairman of the **Drafting Committee** (The 'Father of the Constitution').
- **Objective Resolution**: Moved by **Jawaharlal Nehru** on Dec 13, 1946; laid the foundation for the Preamble.
- **Adoption Date**: **Nov 26, 1949** (Constitution Day).
- **Commencement Date**: **Jan 26, 1950** (Republic Day).

---

## Details / Notes

### Composition of the Constituent Assembly
- Total strength: **389** (Initially); **299** (After Partition).
- Representation: Provinces (296) and Princely States (93).
- Seat allocation: Based on **population** (roughly 1 seat per million).
- Elections: Indirectly by members of the Provincial Legislative Assemblies.

### Important Committees (Prelims Hot-spots)
1. **Drafting Committee**: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (7 members).
2. **Union Powers/Constitution Committee**: Jawaharlal Nehru.
3. **Provincial Constitution Committee**: Sardar Patel.
4. **Rules of Procedure/Steering Committee**: Dr. Rajendra Prasad.
5. **Advisory Committee on Fundamental Rights/Minorities**: Sardar Patel.

### Sources of the Constitution (Borrowed Parts)
- **Government of India Act 1935**: Federal Scheme, Office of Governor, Judiciary, Emergency Provisions (~75% of Constitution).
- **British Constitution**: Parliamentary system, Rule of Law, Single Citizenship, Cabinet system.
- **US Constitution**: Fundamental Rights, Judicial Review, Preamble, Removal of SC/HC judges.
- **Irish Constitution**: Directive Principles (DPSP), Nomination of members to Rajya Sabha.
- **Canadian Constitution**: Federation with a strong Centre, Residuary powers with Centre.
- **Australian Constitution**: Concurrent List, Joint sitting of the two Houses.
- **Weimar Constitution (Germany)**: Suspension of Fundamental Rights during Emergency.
- **Soviet Constitution (USSR)**: Fundamental Duties, Ideals of Justice (Social, Economic, Political) in Preamble.
- **French Constitution**: Ideals of Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity.
