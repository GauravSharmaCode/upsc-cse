# Polity - Fundamental Rights

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
Fundamental Rights are enshrined in **Part III** (Articles 12–35) of the Constitution. Known as the **'Magna Carta' of India**, they are essential for the overall development of individuals and the protection of their dignity.

## Key points

- **Justiciable**: They are enforceable by courts (Article 32 and 226).
- **Not Absolute**: They are subject to **reasonable restrictions**.
- **Suspension**: Can be suspended during a National Emergency (except **Articles 20 and 21**).
- **Citizen-only Rights**: Articles **15, 16, 19, 29, and 30** are available ONLY to Indian citizens.
- **Writs**: The Supreme Court (Art 32) and High Courts (Art 226) can issue writs to enforce these rights.

---

## Details / Notes

### Six Categories of Fundamental Rights
1. **Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)**:
    - Art 14: Equality before law and Equal protection of laws.
    - Art 15: Prohibition of discrimination (Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of Birth).
    - Art 16: Equality of opportunity in public employment.
    - Art 17: Abolition of **Untouchability**.
    - Art 18: Abolition of **Titles**.
2. **Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)**:
    - Art 19: Six basic freedoms (Speech, Assembly, Association, Movement, Residence, Profession).
    - Art 20: Protection in respect of conviction for offences.
    - Art 21: **Right to Life and Personal Liberty**.
    - Art 21A: Right to Education (introduced by 86th Amendment, 2002).
    - Art 22: Protection against arrest and detention.
3. **Right against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)**:
    - Art 23: Prohibition of human trafficking and forced labour (begar).
    - Art 24: Prohibition of employment of children in factories.
4. **Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)**:
    - Art 25: Freedom of conscience and profession, practice, and propagation.
5. **Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)**:
    - Art 29: Protection of interests of minorities.
    - Art 30: Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions.
6. **Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)**:
    - Allows individuals to move the SC for enforcement of FRs. Dr. Ambedkar called it the **'Heart and Soul of the Constitution'**.

### Important Articles for Prelims
- **Article 12**: Definition of 'State'.
- **Article 13**: Laws inconsistent with FRs are void (Basis of **Judicial Review**).
- **Article 33–35**: Application of FRs to Armed Forces, Martial Law, and Legislation.

> [!WARNING]
> The **Right to Property** (formerly Art 31) was removed from the list of FRs by the **44th Amendment Act, 1978**. It is now a **Legal Right** under **Article 300-A** in Part XII.
