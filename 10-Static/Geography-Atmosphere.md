# Geography - Atmosphere (Short, Exam‑Ready Notes)

---

## Summary

The atmosphere is the **gaseous blanket around Earth**, organized into layers that regulate climate, weather, and radiation.

---

## Key Facts

- **99% of atmospheric mass** lies within **32 km** of Earth.
- **Stratosphere** has the ozone layer; temperature rises with height.
- **Mesosphere** is the coldest layer; meteors burn here.
- **Thermosphere** hosts auroras, ISS, and very high temperatures.

---

## Composition of the Atmosphere

### Major Gases (Constant)

- **Nitrogen (78%)**: Inert, controls combustion, essential for nitrogen cycle.
- **Oxygen (21%)**: Respiration, combustion.
- **Argon (0.93%)**: Inert gas from decay of potassium.

### Variable Gases (Climate Drivers)

- **Water Vapour (0–4%)**: Weather, clouds, greenhouse effect.
- **CO₂ (~0.04%)**: Strong absorber of outgoing heat; key for greenhouse warming.
- **Methane & N₂O**: Potent GHGs; both rising due to human activity.

### Ozone (O₃)

- **Stratospheric Ozone (Good)**: Absorbs 97–99% UV radiation.
- **Tropospheric Ozone (Bad)**: Pollutant + greenhouse gas.

### Ozone-Depleting Substances (ODS)

- **CFCs, HCFCs, Halons**: Major human-made ozone destroyers.
- **Bromine**: 60× more potent than chlorine.
- **Natural Sources**: Methyl chloride/bromide; oceanic emissions.

### Aerosols / Particulates

- **Sources**: Dust, sea salt, smoke, volcanic ash.
- **Functions**: Cloud formation, scattering sunlight.
- **High concentration** in the troposphere.

---

## Vertical Structure of the Atmosphere

### 1. Troposphere (0–12 km)

- All weather occurs here. Height varies: **8 km (poles)** to **17–20 km (equator)**.
- **Temperature decreases** with height.
- **Normal Lapse Rate**: **6.5°C/km**.
- Ends at the **Tropopause**.

### 2. Stratosphere (12–50 km)

- Ozone absorbs UV, causing **temperature increase**.
- Very **stable**, little vertical mixing.
- Jets fly here.
- Ends at **Stratopause**.

### 3. Mesosphere (50–80 km)

- **Temperature decreases** with height.
- Coldest layer; meteors burn.
- Ends at **Mesopause**.

### 4. Thermosphere (80–700 km)

- **Temperature increases sharply**.
- Contains the **ionosphere**, essential for radio communication.
- ISS orbits here; auroras occur.

### 5. Exosphere (700–10,000 km)

- Outer boundary blending into space.
- Extremely low density; hydrogen and helium escape.
