# Polity - Parliament of India

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
The Parliament is the supreme legislative body of India, enshrined in **Part V** (Articles 79–122). It follows the **Westminster model** of government and consists of the **President**, the **Lok Sabha**, and the **Rajya Sabha**.

## Key points

- **Composition**: Article 79 states that Parliament = **President + Rajya Sabha + Lok Sabha**.
- **The President**: Not a member of either House but an integral part (assent is required for bills).
- **Rajya Sabha (Council of States)**: **Permanent body**; not subject to dissolution. Members represent States and UTs.
- **Lok Sabha (House of the People)**: **Temporary body**; elected for 5 years. Members represent the people directly.
- **Quorum**: 1/10th of the total membership of each House (Article 100).

---

## Details / Notes

### 1. Rajya Sabha (Upper House)
- Max strength: **250** (Currently 245).
- Nomination: **12 members** nominated by President (Literature, Science, Art, Social Service).
- Elections: Indirect, using **Proportional Representation** (Single Transferable Vote).
- Tenure: **6 years**; 1/3rd members retire every 2nd year.

### 2. Lok Sabha (Lower House)
- Max strength: **550** (Currently 543).
- Elections: Direct election (Universal Adult Franchise).
- **Note**: The nomination of 2 Anglo-Indians was abolished by the **104th Amendment Act, 2019**.
- Tenure: **5 years**; can be dissolved earlier by the President.

### 3. Officers of Parliament
- **Speaker of Lok Sabha**: Presides over the House. Does not vote in first instance but has a **casting vote** in case of a tie.
- **Chairman of Rajya Sabha**: The **Vice-President of India** is the ex-officio Chairman.

### 4. Important Parliamentary Terms
- **Adjournment**: Suspends the sitting for a specific time.
- **Prorogation**: Terminates a session of the House (done by President).
- **Dissolution**: Ends the life of the Lok Sabha (done by President).
- **Joint Sitting (Article 108)**: To resolve a deadlock between the two Houses. Presided over by the **Speaker**.

### 5. Types of Bills
1. **Ordinary Bills**: Can be introduced in either House.
2. **Money Bills (Article 110)**: Only in **Lok Sabha** on the recommendation of the President. Speaker's decision is final.
3. **Financial Bills**: Categorized as I and II.
4. **Constitutional Amendment Bills (Article 368)**: Does NOT allow a joint sitting.

> [!NOTE]
> The **Budget** (Annual Financial Statement) is mentioned in **Article 112**. It must be presented before the Parliament before the start of the financial year.
