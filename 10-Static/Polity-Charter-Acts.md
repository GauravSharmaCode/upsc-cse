# Artifact: The Charter Acts (1793–1853)

The Charter Acts were passed by the British Parliament to renew the East India Company's (EIC) charter every 20 years, gradually stripping the Company of its commercial privileges and asserting the Crown's sovereignty.

## Evolution of the Charter Acts

### 1. [[Charter Act of 1793]]
- Extended the Company's privileges for 20 years.
- Royal approval made mandatory for appointments.

### 2. [[Charter Act of 1813]]
- **End of Monopoly**: EIC's monopoly over Indian trade ended (except for trade in tea and trade with China).
- **Education**: Allotted ₹1 Lakh for the promotion of Indian education.
- Allowed Christian missionaries to come to India.

### 3. [[Charter Act of 1833]] (Saint Helena Act)
- **Final Centralization**: Made the Governor-General of Bengal the [[Governor General of India]].
- **Commercial End**: EIC became a purely administrative body; its commercial activities ended.
- Attempted to introduce open competition for Civil Services (negated by the Court of Directors).

### 4. [[Charter Act of 1853]]
- **Legislative/Executive Split**: Separated the legislative and executive functions of the Governor-General’s Council.
- **Open Competition**: Finally introduced open competition for Civil Services (Macaulay Committee, 1854).
- No fixed period for the Company's charter (signaling imminent takeover).
