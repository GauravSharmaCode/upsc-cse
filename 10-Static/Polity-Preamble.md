# Polity - Preamble to the Constitution

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
The Preamble serves as an introduction to the Constitution. It embodies the basic philosophy and fundamental values—political, moral, and religious—on which the Constitution is based. It is described as the **'Identity Card of the Constitution'** (by N.A. Palkhivala).

## Key points

- **Objective Resolution**: The Preamble is based on the Objective Resolution drafted and moved by **Jawaharlal Nehru** (Adopted Jan 22, 1947).
- **Source of Authority**: "We, the People of India" indicates that the people are the source of power.
- **Amendment**: The Preamble has been amended only **once** by the **42nd Amendment Act (1976)**, which added three new words: **Socialist, Secular, and Integrity**.
- **Not Justiciable**: Its provisions are not enforceable in a court of law.
- **Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)**: The SC held that the Preamble is a **part of the Constitution** and can be amended under Article 368, provided the **'Basic Structure'** is not destroyed.

---

## Details / Notes

### Key Words in the Preamble
1. **Sovereign**: India is an independent state; not a dominion of any other power.
2. **Socialist**: Indian socialism is 'Democratic Socialism' (Mixed Economy), not 'Communistic Socialism'.
3. **Secular**: The state has no official religion; all religions have equal status and support.
4. **Democratic**: Rule by the people; India has an indirect (parliamentary) democracy.
5. **Republic**: The head of the state (President) is elected, not hereditary.
6. **Justice**: Social, Economic, and Political (Borrowed from the **Russian Revolution**).
7. **Liberty**: Of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship (Borrowed from the **French Revolution**).
8. **Equality**: Of status and of opportunity.
9. **Fraternity**: Assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the nation.

### Legal Status of the Preamble
- **Berubari Union Case (1960)**: SC held that Preamble is **NOT** a part of the Constitution.
- **Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)**: SC **reversed** the Berubari opinion; held that Preamble **IS** a part of the Constitution.
- **LIC of India Case (1995)**: SC again held that Preamble is an **integral part** of the Constitution.

> [!NOTE]
> The Preamble is neither a source of power to the legislature nor a prohibition upon the powers of the legislature.
