# Polity - Preamble to the Constitution

**Summary**
The Preamble is the introductory statement that reflects the philosophy, values, and aspirations on which the Indian Constitution is founded. While not conferring enforceable rights, it acts as a guiding light for interpreting the Constitution's provisions and understanding the framers' intent.

## Key Facts

- **Origin**: Based on the **'Objectives Resolution'** drafted and moved by **Jawaharlal Nehru** (Moved: Dec 13, 1946; Adopted: Jan 22, 1947).
- **Source of Authority**: Derived from the people ("We, the People of India").
- **Adoption vs Enforcement**: Adopted on **Nov 26, 1949** (Constitution Day), enforced on **Jan 26, 1950**.
- **Amendment**: Amended only **once** by the **42nd Amendment Act (1976)**. It added three words: **Socialist, Secular, and Integrity**.
- **Justiciability**: It is **non-justiciable** (not enforceable in courts). It is neither a source of power to the legislature nor a prohibition upon its powers.

---

## Details / Notes

### Core Elements & Objectives
1. **Nature of the State**: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic.
2. **Justice**: Social, Economic, and Political (Borrowed from the **Russian Revolution**). 
    - *Note*: Economic Justice is found in both the Preamble and the DPSP.
3. **Liberty**: Of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship (Borrowed from the **French Revolution**).
    - *Trap*: Does NOT include "Economic liberty".
4. **Equality**: Of status and of opportunity.
    - Promoted through civic (Art 14–18), political (Art 325, 326), and economic (DPSP) provisions.
5. **Fraternity**: Assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the nation.

### Borrowed Features in the Preamble
- **USA**: The concept of a written preamble and the opening phrase "We, the People".
- **France**: Ideals of Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity.
- **USSR (Russia)**: Ideals of Justice (Social, Economic, Political).

### Legal Status & Supreme Court Judgments
- **Berubari Union Case (1960)**: SC held that the Preamble is a key to the minds of the makers but is **NOT** a part of the Constitution.
- **Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)**: SC reversed its earlier stance, declaring that the Preamble **IS** a part of the Constitution. It established the **'Basic Structure Doctrine'**, meaning the Preamble can be amended, but its core features cannot be destroyed.
- **LIC of India Case (1995)**: SC reaffirmed the Preamble as an **integral part** of the Constitution, but clarified it is not directly enforceable in a court of justice.

### Famous Quotes (MCQ Match the Following)
- **N.A. Palkhivala**: "Identity card of the Constitution"
- **K.M. Munshi**: "Horoscope of our sovereign democratic republic"
- **Pandit Thakurdas Bhargava**: "Soul of the Constitution"

> [!NOTE]
> **42nd Amendment Act (1976) specifics**: Besides adding "Socialist" and "Secular", it changed the phrase "unity of the nation" to "unity and integrity of the Nation".

> [!TIP]
> **Mind of the Makers**: If a PYQ asks where the "mind of the makers" of the Constitution is reflected, the answer is always the **Preamble**.
