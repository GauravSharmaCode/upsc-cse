# Geography - Insolation and Heat Budget

**Summary (2–3 lines)**
Insolation (Incoming Solar Radiation) is the energy received by Earth from the Sun. Earth maintains a constant temperature by radiating back the same amount of energy it receives, a process known as the Earth's **Heat Budget**.

## Key Facts

- **Solar Constant**: ~2 calories per square cm per minute (1.94 langleys) at the top of the atmosphere.
- **Albedo**: The percentage of solar radiation reflected back without heating the surface. Earth's average albedo is **~35%**.
- **Terrestrial Radiation**: Earth radiates energy in the form of **long-wave radiation**, which is absorbed by greenhouse gases like CO₂.
- **Perihelion**: Earth is closest to the Sun (Jan 3). Insolation is slightly higher.
- **Aphelion**: Earth is farthest from the Sun (July 4). Insolation is slightly lower.

---

## Details / Notes

### Factors Affecting Insolation
1. **The angle of inclination of the sun’s rays**: Slanting rays cover more area and are less intense.
2. **The length of the day**: Longer days = more insolation.
3. **The transparency of the atmosphere**: Clouds, dust, and moisture absorb/scatter radiation.
4. **The configuration of land (Relief)**: South-facing slopes in the Northern Hemisphere receive more direct sun.

### Heating and Cooling of Atmosphere
- **Conduction**: Heat transfer by direct contact (limited to lower layers).
- **Convection**: Vertical transfer of heat by moving air (main heating mechanism in troposphere).
- **Advection**: Horizontal transfer of heat by winds. **Advection is more significant than convection** for global temperature distribution.
- **Radiation**: Long-wave terrestrial radiation heats the atmosphere from below.

### The Earth's Heat Budget (Roughly)
- **Total Incoming**: 100 units.
- **Reflected (Albedo)**: ~35 units (27 from clouds, 6 from atmosphere, 2 from surface).
- **Absorbed by Atmosphere**: ~14 units.
- **Absorbed by Earth's Surface**: ~51 units.
- **Total Radiated Back**: 100 units (65 from atmosphere/space, 35 reflected).
